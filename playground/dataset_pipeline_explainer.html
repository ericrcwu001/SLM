STAGE 01

Collection

Every candidate comes from a declared source with recorded provenance. Eight sources feed the corpus, from curated expert photo sets to a broad web discovery sweep, plus a controlled procedural generator for coverage gaps.

A single machine-readable inventory names every source, its family, and how it was accessed. Each raw item lands in a provenance registry, one line per candidate.

STAGE 02

One canonical form

Sources arrive in different formats, grid sizes, and color spaces. Before any comparison is meaningful, everything is resampled to a single representation so that a difference in the data means a difference in the color, not the container.

LUTs authored in wider spaces are color-managed into the canonical domain. Anything whose source color space cannot be resolved is recorded and rejected, never guessed.

Some sources are not LUTs at all. Expert photo pairs are turned into a LUT by fitting a global transform to the source-and-target images, then validating the fit against held-out pixels. Only fits that hold up are kept.

STAGE 03

Quality gates sort every LUT into three tiers

Two gate layers run on the canonical LUT. Representability asks whether a global transform can faithfully reproduce the look. Safety and quality ask whether the result is well behaved: no clipping, no folding, no skin-tone damage. The verdict is a tier.

ΔE00 (CIEDE2000) is a perceptual color-difference metric. Lower means closer to the intended look. A dedicated skin-locus gate protects skin tones, which the eye judges harshly.

STAGE 04

Leakage-safe splits

Survivors are grouped into split units by how similar their color transforms are, so near-identical LUTs cannot land on both sides of the train and evaluation boundary. Without this, scores look better than the model really is.

STAGE 05

Teaching the model what to say, and when to refuse

Each kept LUT needs a natural-language instruction. A vision-capable teacher model writes it from the measured look and the source image. A second model checks the language. Crucially, the teacher never sets the facts: a deterministic behavior measurement is the authority for every color claim.

A vector-quantized encoder is frozen and shared across the set. It reconstructs the held-out LUTs at a mean ΔE00 of 1.31 and mean PSNR near 37 dB, so the compact target loses almost nothing the eye can see.

STAGE 07

Staging and delivery

The finished corpus is packed into verifiable shards and published to a durable, private dataset root, then pulled down and checked byte for byte wherever training runs.

Pack

Raw LUTs, canonical residuals, registries, splits, and the frozen tokenizer are bundled into structure-preserving tar shards, each with a manifest listing member counts, bytes, and hashes.

5 shards · ~9.85 GB

Publish

Shards sync to a private Hugging Face dataset root as the durable source of truth. Packing is idempotent by content, so re-runs never duplicate work.

private dataset root

Verify

On the training machine, every shard is pulled, checked against its sha256, and extracted with resume markers. A corrupted or partial shard cannot slip into a run.

sha256 verified

Plain definitions

Glossary

The terms used throughout, defined the way they are used in this project.

A table that remaps every input color to an output color, applied to the whole image at once. It is a global color grade. It cannot target a region, change content, or relight a scene. That boundary is the whole supported-versus-unsupported distinction.
The text format that stores a LUT. Here it is standardized to 17 samples per axis, which is 4,913 color nodes, in sRGB at a D65 white point with trilinear interpolation between nodes.
CIEDE2000, a perceptual measure of how far apart two colors look. Lower is closer to the target. It is the fidelity metric used at fit acceptance, at the quality gates, and when checking the tokenizer.
Peak signal-to-noise ratio, in decibels, comparing a reconstructed LUT to the original. Higher is better. Used to confirm the compact token representation stays faithful.
The gate verdict. Gold is a faithful global fit and is the only tier eligible for headline scoring. Diagnostic is usable for training but outside gold bounds. Rejected fails a threshold and is dropped.
A set of measured color deltas probed from a LUT: temperature, tint, exposure, contrast, saturation, shadow and highlight shifts, skin-tone effect, and smoothness. This measurement, not the teacher model, is the authority for every factual claim in an instruction.
A vision-capable frontier model that writes the natural-language instruction from the source image and the measured look. A second model checks the language. Neither is allowed to override the measured facts.
A vector-quantized encoder compresses a LUT into 64 discrete tokens drawn from a 256-entry codebook. This is what the model learns to emit for a supported request, instead of thousands of raw numbers.
For a request a single global LUT cannot satisfy, the correct answer is a literal refusal. The dataset teaches this on purpose, including mixed prompts that combine a valid change with an impossible one.
Training the model on pairs of input (image plus instruction) and target (LUT tokens or a refusal). The active set of 3,033 rows is exactly this training corpus.
